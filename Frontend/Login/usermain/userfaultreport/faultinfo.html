When to file a report

Hawker Stalk App - When to File a Fault Report

Welcome to the Hawker Stalk Fault Reporting system. This documentation provides a comprehensive guide on when to file a fault report, the types of faults you can report, and best practices to ensure your report is accurate and effective.

1. Purpose of the Fault Reporting System

The Fault Reporting System in the Hawker Stalk App is designed to improve the quality and functionality of hawker centers by allowing users to report issues directly. Your feedback is invaluable in ensuring a safe, hygienic, and enjoyable experience for everyone. Filing a report enables us to take swift action and maintain high standards across all hawker centers.

2. Types of Faults to Report

2.1. Cleanliness Issues

  • Unhygienic Food Preparation: If you observe food being prepared in an unsanitary manner, such as improper handling of ingredients or equipment that isn't cleaned properly.
  • Dirty Tables or Chairs: Report any tables, chairs, or shared surfaces that are visibly dirty or stained.
  • Overflowing Trash Bins: If trash bins are full and refuse is spilling out, impacting the cleanliness of the environment.
  • Pest Sightings: Sightings of rodents, cockroaches, or other pests in the hawker center.

2.2. Infrastructure Issues

  • Damaged or Broken Furniture: If tables, chairs, or benches are broken or pose a risk to users.
  • Leaking or Broken Taps: Faulty or leaking taps in the washing areas or restrooms.
  • Non-Functioning Fans or Ventilation: If fans, air conditioning, or ventilation systems are not working properly, impacting air circulation.
  • Electrical Issues: Exposed wiring, non-functioning lights, or any electrical issues that may pose a safety hazard.
  • Slippery Floors: Wet or slippery floors that are not attended to and could lead to accidents.

2.3. Food Quality Concerns

  • Expired or Stale Ingredients: If you suspect that a stall is using expired or stale ingredients, especially in perishable items like seafood or dairy.
  • Improper Storage of Ingredients: If ingredients are not stored properly, increasing the risk of contamination.
  • Poor Food Handling Practices: Such as handling cash and food without hand washing, or using the same utensils for raw and cooked foods.

2.4. Stall-Specific Issues

  • Unauthorized Price Hikes: If a stall is charging prices significantly higher than the listed prices or engaging in deceptive practices.
  • Refusal to Follow Health Guidelines: If a stall does not adhere to health regulations, such as wearing masks, gloves, or following proper sanitization.
  • Customer Service Issues: Rude or disrespectful behavior from stall staff.

2.5. Accessibility Issues

  • Obstructed Pathways: If there are obstacles blocking pathways that hinder movement, especially for individuals with disabilities.
  • Non-Functioning Elevators or Ramps: If elevators, ramps, or other accessibility features are out of order or poorly maintained.

3. When Should You File a Fault Report?

Filing a fault report is essential when an issue is significant enough to affect public safety, health, or the overall dining experience. Here’s a guide to help you determine if you should file a report:

3.1. Immediate Danger to Public Safety

If the fault poses an immediate risk to the safety of patrons (e.g., exposed wiring, fire hazards, or slippery floors), it should be reported immediately to prevent accidents.

3.2. Repeated Incidents

If you notice recurring issues that are not being addressed, such as trash bins that are consistently overflowing, report the issue. Continuous faults indicate a lack of maintenance and require prompt attention.

3.3. Health & Hygiene Violations

Health-related issues, like unhygienic food preparation, pest sightings, or expired ingredients, directly affect the well-being of diners and should be reported without delay.

3.4. Disruption of Normal Operations

If an issue disrupts the normal operation of the hawker center—such as non-functional fans during hot weather, broken seating, or unavailable washing areas—it is recommended to file a report. This will alert maintenance teams to resolve the issue quickly.

3.5. Customer Experience Impact

While minor inconveniences can often be overlooked, issues that significantly affect the customer experience (e.g., unresponsive or rude stall staff, mispriced items) should be reported. This helps management maintain high standards of service and ensures fair pricing practices.

4. Best Practices for Filing a Fault Report

4.1. Be Specific and Detailed

Provide as much detail as possible, including:

  • Exact Location: Specify the stall number, section, or nearby landmarks.
  • Time and Date: Mention when you observed the fault. This helps in tracking down intermittent issues.
  • Description of the Issue: Describe the issue clearly, such as "Trash bin overflowing near Stall 7 in Section B."

4.2. Use Visual Evidence

Whenever possible, attach photos of the fault. Visual evidence supports your report and helps maintenance teams better understand the issue.

4.3. Classify the Severity of the Issue

Some apps may allow you to classify the fault based on its severity (e.g., low, medium, high). This prioritization helps in assigning urgent tasks to maintenance staff.

4.4. Provide Follow-Up Information

If the issue persists after a report, consider following up or filing an additional report. Continuous updates help the maintenance team to understand the scope and duration of unresolved issues.

5. Examples of Effective Fault Reports

  • Example 1: Safety Hazard
    Report Title: "Exposed Wiring Near Sink in Section C"
    Description: "There is an exposed electrical wire near the washing area in Section C. This could pose a safety risk, especially for children."
  • Example 2: Cleanliness Issue
    Report Title: "Overflowing Trash Bin by Stall 10"
    Description: "The trash bin next to Stall 10 has been full since this morning, and trash is starting to accumulate around it. Please have it emptied."
  • Example 3: Food Quality Concern
    Report Title: "Expired Seafood at Stall 5"
    Description: "I noticed a foul smell coming from the seafood at Stall 5. The prawns appeared discolored, and there is a risk of food poisoning if consumed."

6. Common Mistakes to Avoid When Filing a Report

  • Being Vague: Avoid vague descriptions like "dirty area" or "bad food." Specific details help address the problem quickly.
  • Using Inappropriate Language: Ensure that the report remains respectful and professional.
  • Reporting Minor Inconveniences: Only file reports for issues that impact safety, health, or the experience of other patrons. Minor inconveniences may not warrant a report.

7. What Happens After You File a Report?

After filing a report, here’s what typically happens:

  • Acknowledgment: You may receive a notification that your report has been received.
  • Review by Maintenance Team: The fault is reviewed, prioritized, and assigned to the relevant personnel.
  • Resolution: Depending on the severity, the fault may be resolved within a few hours or days.
  • Follow-Up: In some cases, you may receive a follow-up notification once the issue has been addressed.
